Talking Pigeon — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Piegeon ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you use the Talking Pigeon mobile application (the "App"). By using the App, you agree to the practices described below.

1. Information We Collect

Microphone & Voice Recordings

Talking Pigeon uses your device's microphone to let you talk to the pigeon character. When you tap to record, the App captures a short audio clip, applies a voice effect, and plays it back to you.

These recordings are processed entirely on your device and are not uploaded to, transmitted to, or stored on our servers. They are temporarily held in memory or local device storage only for as long as needed to play back the effect, and are discarded afterward. (If a future update changes this — for example, by adding cloud-based voice processing — this policy will be updated to reflect that before the change ships.)

You can deny or revoke microphone access at any time in your device settings. If you do, voice-based features of the App will not work, but you can still use the rest of the App.

Age Verification

The first time you open the App, you're asked to enter your date of birth (month, day, year) via a neutral screen — the same screen, with the same wording, for every player. Nothing on this screen hints that any particular answer unlocks anything.

Your birth date is used once, locally on your device, to calculate whether you're under 13. The birth date itself is never saved anywhere — only the result of that calculation (a true/false flag) is stored locally on your device, together with a flag noting that the screen has been completed. Returning players aren't asked again. If this check can't be completed for any reason, the App defaults to treating the session as a child's for ad purposes — it never assumes you're older than 13 by default.

This flag determines which ad experience you see, as described in Section 4 (Children's Privacy). No ads of any kind are requested or shown until this screen has been completed.

We do not require account creation, and the App does not collect your name, email address, or other directly identifying information. We do not use cloud save or in-app purchases in this App.

3. How We Use Information & Legal Basis

Any data described above is used solely to operate the App's voice playback feature, to display and measure advertising, and to diagnose and fix technical issues. We do not sell your personal information.

Where GDPR applies (EEA/UK users), we and our ad partners rely on the following legal bases:

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) — for personalized/targeted advertising, any non-essential tracking identifiers, and for microphone access itself, which your device requests permission for before first use. Applies only to players 13 and over.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) — for non-personalized (contextual) advertising needed to keep the App free, and for crash/diagnostic logs needed to keep the App functioning correctly.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR) — for the age screen described in Section 1, which exists to comply with COPPA and Google Play's Families policy. This processing isn't optional or consent-based, since it's how we determine and apply the legally required child-safe ad experience.

The App does not use any data described above to make solely automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects on you. Ad selection may involve automated, algorithm-based targeting by our ad partners, but this does not amount to profiling with legal effect.

4. Children's Privacy

Talking Pigeon has a mixed audience: it's designed to be enjoyed by both children under 13 and older players. Because of this, the App uses a neutral age screen (described in Section 1) to determine which ad experience applies to each player, in line with Google Play's Families policy and the US Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).

  • If you're under 13 (or if age couldn't be determined): the App tags every ad request as child-directed. This disables personalized advertising, remarketing, and interest-based ads; prevents your device's advertising ID from being transmitted; and restricts ads to Google Play's Families Self-Certified Ads SDK sources only. This signal is set via our ad mediation platform (Unity LevelPlay), which propagates it to every mediated ad network, including AdMob.
  • If you're 13 or over: standard (non-child-directed) ads may be shown, as described in Section 1.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 beyond the minimal, non-identifying data described in Section 1. As noted there, voice recordings are processed entirely on-device and never leave the App, regardless of age, and device/advertising identifiers are never collected for child sessions. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information beyond what's described here, please contact us using the details in Section 10 and we will address it.

5. Data Retention

Voice recordings are not retained after playback, as described above. The only data related to the age screen that's ever stored is two true/false flags saved locally on your device (whether the screen was completed, and whether the session is treated as a child's); your actual birth date is discarded immediately after that calculation and is never transmitted anywhere. Advertising identifiers, app interaction data, and diagnostic data are retained by our active ad mediation and diagnostic partners (currently AdMob and Unity Ads) according to their own retention policies. Local data stored on your device, including the age-screen flags, can be cleared at any time by uninstalling the App or clearing the App's data via your device settings — doing so will show the age screen again on next launch.

6. Data Security

All data collected by the App and its third-party SDKs is encrypted in transit using industry-standard protocols (TLS). We take reasonable measures to protect the App and the limited data it processes, but no method of electronic processing or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

  • Erasure — request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten").
